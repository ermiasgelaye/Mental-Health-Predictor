Project Background

Mental health is a major global health concern. Understanding patterns in mental health indicators can help inform prevention strategies, guide resource allocation, and support public health planning.

This project uses cross-country data to explore relationships between mental health indicators, socio-economic context, and suicide rates. The focus is on identifying risk indicators at a population level and translating findings into prevention-oriented insights.

Research Questions:

  • Which mental health and social indicators are most strongly associated with suicide rates across countries?
  • How do socio-economic and demographic factors relate to differences in suicide rates?
  • How do patterns vary across regions and over time?
  • How can statistical and machine learning models support prevention-oriented insights at the population level?

Note: This analysis is intended for population-level understanding and prevention planning. It is not designed to predict individual behavior or provide clinical risk assessments.
