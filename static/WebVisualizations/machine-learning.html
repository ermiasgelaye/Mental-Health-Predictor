1. Introduction

In this project we used various machine learning models to train and test data for predicting suicide mortality rates from various macro socio-economic factors at the national level. We employed multiple algorithms to compare their effectiveness and identify the most significant predictors.

5. Conclusion

Mental health and suicide are both important cases to study because we continue to lose many lives to suicide worldwide. Our project focuses on identifying macro-level socio-economic factors that contribute to this complex problem.

Key Findings
  • Social insurance coverage shows positive correlation with suicide rates
  • Basic education levels negatively correlate with suicide rates
  • SVM achieved best prediction accuracy at 72.2%
  • Complex socio-economic relationships require nuanced interpretation
Challenges & Future Work
  • Data quality issues with many null values
  • Complex individual factors beyond macro-level analysis
  • Need for collaborative, comprehensive approaches
  • Potential for improved models with more data

Future Directions: We believe that if socio-economic factors and policies toward mental health were changed systematically, suicide prevention could be improved. However, the issue remains complex and requires individual-level considerations alongside macro-level analysis.
